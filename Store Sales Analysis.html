Project: Grocery Store Sales Dashboard 

Overview

The Grocery Store Sales Dashboard is a dynamic 
and interactive Power BI report that provides key 
insights into the sales performance of a superma
rket chain. The dashboard is designed for business
 stakeholders and decisionmakers, offering an over
view of sales, profit, customer activity, and store
 performance across various regions. This report
 enables users to analyze data across multiple 
dimensions, such as time, location, and product 
brand, facilitating informed, datadriven decisions.

Key Metrics  
Quantity Sold: Displays the total number of items sold within the selected timeframe, with an indicator showing percentage change.
Total Profit: Shows the overall profit generated, along with a percentage change compared to the previous period.
Total Transactions: Counts the number of completed transactions and highlights growth or decline.
Total Revenue: Calculates the total revenue generated from sales, with a dynamic comparison percentage.
Total Cost: Represents the total cost incurred, along with a comparison to the previous period.
Average Daily Revenue: Provides an average revenue per day based on sales data, useful for gauging daily performance.
Year-over-Year (YoY) Profit: Compares profit from the current year to the previous year to understand annual growth.
Customers This Month: Counts unique customers visiting within the selected month, with an option to view total visitors.
Top 5 High Profit Stores: Highlights the top-performing stores based on profit, with a toggle to view bottom-performing stores.
Regional Performance Map: A visual representation of store locations and their sales performance by region.

Data Model Overview:
The data model is designed with a Snowflake Schema structure, which includes the following tables:
f_transactions: The fact table containing transactional data with fields such as transaction_date, customer_id, store_id, quantity, total_cost,
 and total_revenue.
d_Calendar: The date dimension table that provides date details for time-based analysis, with fields like Date, MonthName, and Year.
d_Stores: Stores dimension table including store information such as store_id, city, region, and store_name.
d_Customers: Contains customer demographics and identifiers to track unique visitors.
Key DAX Measures:
The project includes several custom DAX measures for insightful analysis:
Total Revenue: Sum of revenue from all transactions.
Total Profit: Sum of profit from all transactions.
Quantity Sold: Sum of items sold across all transactions.
YOY Revenue Growth: Calculates year-over-year revenue growth.
Customers This Month: Displays the unique count of customers for the selected month.
Average Daily Revenue: Calculates the average revenue per day over the selected month or period.
M_Today's Quantity: Shows the quantity sold today, with flexibility to display monthly averages by default.

Visualizations:
KPI Cards: The top section of the dashboard features KPI cards that summarize key metrics, providing a quick overview of store performance.
 Metrics like revenue, profit, and transactions are shown with color-coded indicators to highlight changes.
Trend Line Chart: Displays revenue over time to highlight trends, spikes, and dips in sales performance. Map Visualization: A geographical heat map showing store locations and performance by region, offering spatial insights into store profitability 
and customer distribution.
Top & Bottom 5 Stores: A bar chart that ranks stores by profit, allowing stakeholders to quickly identify top-performing and underperforming locations.
Detailed Table: Displays transaction-level details for deeper analysis, with sorting and filtering options on metrics like quantity, revenue, and profit.
